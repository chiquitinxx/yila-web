Zero Zero AI

Created June 2026, by Jorge Franco

In 007 Skyfall, there is a conversation between Bond and the new Q:

Q: I can do more damage on my laptop sitting in my pijamas before my first cup of Earl Grey than you can do in a year in the field.

Bond: Oh, so why do you need me?

Q: Every now and then, a trigger has to be pulled.

Bond: Or not pulled... It's hard to know which in your pijamas.

To me, this is a fantastic example of seniority, that applies very well to my profession as a software engineer, and also relates to the current situation of the profession with AI adoption. Because it's important to know what this profession is, starting with the name: coder, developer, programmer,... I think these days, when AI is trending, software engineer fits better, because it's really what companies are paying for, in the past, now, and in the future.

When I started my career, writing as much code as possible was my target. At the beginning, I didn't care about many important topics, I just wanted to do as much as possible, learning by doing. And it's good; you need to learn, you have to fail. Leaving the "juniority" means spending time getting experience. While you are getting experience, and you start to revisit your code, or code created by others, you start to discover the important topics. Testing, readability, coupling, complexity, domain, people, maintenance, migrations, performance,... are a few of them. You start to realize that coding itself is not so important, and you become a software engineer.

Now, let's see what AI can do, apart from telling you how amazing your questions are. AI can give you a response, or provide code, from a lot of data in a model, your input, and context. It can do pretty well there, and it will improve over time. I'm very impressed with the results. But, you see, it's just code; for sure a lot better than the code I wrote at the beginning of my career, but nothing more than code, a very small part of what it means to be a software engineer. So here AI provides the first zero, giving just code and stopping real people from growing.

To be a good senior engineer is not easy. It requires that you have good skills, that you meet people to learn from, work on different projects and technologies, and never stop learning. You need good luck and critical thinking,... and many more things. That is the reason some companies pay a lot of money to get the best software engineers. Maybe you think that these engineers write a lot of code, but you can imagine that it's not true. And it's not true now with AI, but also in the past when you had to write complex code by hand. So, there are a few good software engineers, and they don't write too much code; they design, think, plan, and guide,... many important things that are reflected in just a few lines of code.

And we have AI that gets the output from a lot of code in the LLM, and in the future it will fill with more AI-generated code. You can imagine that each day, it will be harder to get code with real value, and the code you get will be less creative, more repetitive and less original. All the software that you can create with AI is just code that anyone can create without effort, a huge amount of code. So, do you really think that code created by AI can be profitable? Maybe, but very difficult, depending on the idea, not the code. Congrats AI for your second zero: you are providing a false feeling of progress, building something with a very low chance of success, and code that is hard to maintain.

Coming back to James Bond: the world still requires people like him, a lot of them, people able to decide how and what to do or what to avoid. The profession of software developer is needed, and I think it will be needed for many years. This double-zero AI is just a tool to amplify the good or bad software engineer that you are.
