Zero Zero AI

Created June 2026, by Jorge Franco

In 007 Skyfall, there is a conversation between Bond and the new Q:

Q: I can do more damage on my laptop sitting in my pijamas before my first cup of Earl Grey than you can do in a year in the field.

Bond: Oh, so why do you need me?

Q: Every now and then, a trigger has to be pulled.

Bond: Or not pulled... It's hard to know which in your pijamas.

To me, this is a fantastic example of seniority, which applies very well to my profession as a software engineer, and also relates to the current situation of the profession with AI adoption.

When I started my career, writing as much code as possible was my target. At the beginning, I just wanted to do as much as possible, learning by doing, ignoring the future of code created. And it's good; you need to learn, you have to fail. Leaving the "juniority" means spending time getting experience. While you are getting experience, and you start to revisit your code, or code created by others, you start to discover the important topics. Testing, readability, coupling, complexity, domain, people, maintenance, continuous delivery, migrations, performance,... are a few of them. You start to realize that coding itself is not so important, and you become a software engineer.

Now, let's see what AI can do, apart from telling you how amazing your questions are. AI can provide an output from one or multiple inputs, using an LLM. It can do pretty well there, and it will improve over time. I'm very impressed with the results. But it's just code; a very small part of what it means to be a software engineer. So here AI’s first ‘zero’, giving code but skipping the learning path on real people.

To be a good senior engineer, it is not easy. It requires that you have good skills, that you meet people to learn from, work on different projects and technologies, and never stop learning. You need good luck and critical thinking,... and many more things. That is the reason some companies pay a lot of money to get the best software engineers. These engineers spend a small portion of their time coding, now with AI, but also in the past when they had to write complex code by hand. They design, think, plan, guide,... many important things that are reflected in just a few lines of code.

In AI, there is no real thinking, applied experience, domain knowledge, creativity, standards, evolution or maintenance in the process of generating code. All the software that you can create with AI can be done by anyone without effort, lines of code from a prompt. So, do you really think that code can be profitable? Maybe, but it's very difficult, depending on the idea, not the code. Congrats AI for your second zero: you are providing a false feeling of progress, building something with a very low chance of success, difficult to evolve and maintain.

If you are a good software engineer, congratulations, AI will amplify you. If you are not there yet, take care, double zero AI can stop your growth.

Coming back to James Bond: the world still requires people like him, a lot of them, people able to decide how and what to do or what to avoid. Bond's software engineers are needed, and I think they will be needed for many years. I have the same feelings from the film scene: innovation is coming to replace us, but for now, the trigger still needs pulling by us. Our profession is changing, making the act of writing code obsolete, but as you've read, it's a small part of what we do.
